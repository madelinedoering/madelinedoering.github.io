Madeline Doering

About

I'm a data enthusiast and math-communicator working to put the femme in STEM and empower people through learning. Currently I'm a Script Editor at Complexly where I work on the educational YouTube channel Crash Course. Here is a sample of my work as writer and editor.

Previously I studied data analytics and warehousing, dynamical systems, and the microfinance industry in Ecuador. When I’m not thinking about math or teaching, I’m reading, baking, looking for whales, or sampling new cheeses. Thanks for stopping by.

Research

The University of British Columbia

I have studied shifts of finite type (SFTs), specifically the sets of periodic points. I also participate in the Dynamical Systems seminar where we have discussed ergodic theory, entropy, and multidimensional shift spaces.

Publications

  1. M.Doering and R. Pavlov. A characterization of the sets of periods within shifts of finite type. INVOLVE, 12:2 (2019).

The University of Denver

During my Master's degree in Business Analytics, I worked with AMP Robotics to complete my capstone project for which I won the Distinguished Capstone Award. This work looked at using system data to predict machine malfunctions in their recycling robot, as well as potential data monetization.

During my undergraduate degree, I wrote honors theses in symbolic dynamics (mathematics) and microfinance (economics). My mathematics thesis was the basis for the "A characterization of the sets of periods within shifts of finite type" with Ronnie Pavlov. My economics thesis, "Microfinance in Ecuador - A Review," was completed onsite with a summer research grant. This work was presented at the 2016 DU Undergraduate Research Symposium .
